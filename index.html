Let's get drunk (on machine learning).

Different models of machine learning were employed to potentially predict human wine taste preferences based on measureable, easily attained chemical properties of wine. Using machine learning techniques such as these can potentially be employed to help in the wine-making process and/or marketing process for target consumers.

The data.

The datasets we used include chemical property measures commonly used to characterize wine as well as quality scores used for wine certification purposes. The data include measures on samples of vinho verde wine (wine from the northwest region of Portugal). Because the properties and taste of red and white wine differ, separate datasets were used for red and white wine samples. Each quality score was the median of at least three scores graded by wine experts who graded each wine sample in a blind taste test on a range from 0 (very bad) to 10 (excellent). For both the red and white wines most quality scores were given a 5 or 6. Below is a histogram of the quality scores.

The following properties were included in the datasets:

fixed acidity (tartaric acid - g / dm^3)

volatile acidity (acetic acid - g / dm^3)

citric acid (g / dm^3)

residual sugar (g / dm^3)

chlorides (sodium chloride - g / dm^3

free sulfur dioxide (mg / dm^3)

total sulfur dioxide (mg / dm^3)

density (g / cm^3)

pH

sulphates (potassium sulphate - g / dm3)

alcohol (% by volume)

quality (score between 0 and 10)

Details about the datasets can be found here. The datasets are publicly available and can be downloaded here.

Source: P. Cortez, A. Cerdeira, F. Almeida, T. Matos and J. Reis. Modeling wine preferences by data mining from physicochemical properties. In Decision Support Systems, Elsevier, 47(4):547-553, 2009.
